design: what makes us unique

we combine simplicity and minimalism with fun and games.

overview

design is at the core of thera_pixel, setting us apart as a therapy option that’s not only effective but also enjoyable—especially for children. by incorporating playful visuals, interactive elements, and game-like experiences, we create an environment that encourages users to actively participate in their therapy sessions.

this thoughtful design not only captures attention but also sustains it, turning repetitive physical therapy exercises into fun challenges. over time, this engagement boosts user retention, making it more likely for patients to consistently follow through with their rehabilitation journeys.

Pixel Art

all of our game graphics are made authentically by us with a pixel art tool, which allows us to color pixel-by-pixel. we chose this design since it is simple to put together and transition from frame to frame throughout the game. this was our first time making a game, and we thought this made the graphics relatively easy!

check it out!

Pico CSS

pico CSS is a minimal, lightweight CSS framework that offers a clean and modern design without relying on heavy libraries or complex class names. we chose Pico CSS for our web design because it provides a sleek, user-friendly interface while keeping the codebase simple and efficient, allowing us to focus on creating an engaging and accessible experience for users of all ages.

we also added various animations and transitions using css. we imported fonts from google's font collection and took time to choose a pallete of taseteful and minimal colors and fonts.

check it out!

joint and connection visualization

we decided to keep a camera window open next to each game to allow users to see their form. we color coded and highlighted certain joints and connections between joints to visualize where one's limbs are in the computer's vision. we did this using mediapipe.

check it out!

our goals

we hope to design more complicated and competitive games, experimenting how different game elements and point systems may affect how users react differently. for this hackathon, we chose to build a pizza game, but in the coming future, we hope to expand to a suite of games for people with different impairments, ages, and interests.
